Projects | PhD Thesis

I am a lead data scientist working in the growth hacking team of WSO2. I recently completed my PhD in the School of Computing and Communications at the Open University, the United Kingdom where my supervisors were Professor Arosha Bandara, Professor Blaine Price and Professor Bashar Nuseibeh. I am also part of the SEAD group and was a member of the STRETCH project group. Continuing my relationship with the Open University, UK, I have become a visiting researcher of the University where I continue my research collaborations.

My primary research interests are Ubiquitous Computing and the Future of Living, Health Informatics, Longevity and Meta-Learning. My PhD thesis topic was Privacy-aware Smart Home Interface Framework (PASHI-Framework), where the framework was capable of developing smart home user interfaces that are capable of adapting their configuration and behaviour to preserve privacy between users in multi-user contexts. During my PhD, I focused on adaptive user interfaces, user modelling, privacy in cyber-physical systems and applied machine learning.

Prior to my PhD, I worked as a Software Engineer at Cambio Software Engineering (2016/17) and I was a Google Summer of Code Mentor (2017) for OpenMRS. I completed my B.Sc in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Moratuwa, Sri Lanka. During my B.Sc I was a Google Summer of Code Intern (2016) and a Google Student Ambassador (2014/15/16). I also interned as a Research Engineering intern at Augmented Human Lab of SUTD-MIT International Design Centre (2014).

Apart from my core-research work, I have founded a not-for-profit organisation, Sustainable Education Foundation (SEF) to empower Sri Lankan students and the education system.
